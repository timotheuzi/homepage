1. Introduction

Hooby Lab is a multi-platform security suite inspired by the Shheissee and Shimmy roadmaps. It provides real-time monitoring of your network, Bluetooth environment, and physical hardware sensors to protect you from digital and physical intrusions.

2. Getting Started

When you first launch the application, it will:

  • Request necessary permissions (Bluetooth, Location, Network).
  • Perform an initial security scan.
  • Establish a baseline for "Known Devices" (once you manually trust them).

Initial Setup Tip: Go to the Devices tab and review the discovered devices. Use the "Trust" button for your own phone, laptop, and peripherals to reduce false positive alerts.

3. The Dashboard

The Dashboard is your command center:

  • Status Header: Green means "System Secure." Red indicates high-severity threats detected in the last minute.
  • System Statistics: Quick glance at the count of discovered devices, active attacks, and network types.
  • Recent Threats: A live-updating list of the 5 most recent security incidents.

4. Device Discovery & Management

Located in the Devices tab, this screen lists all entities found nearby:

  • Network Devices: Identified by IP address.
  • Bluetooth Devices: Identified by MAC address and Name.
  • AirTags: Specifically flagged if Apple-compatible BLE tracking tokens are found.
  • Trusting Devices: Tap "Trust" to whitelist a device. This prevents "Unknown Device" alerts in future scans.

5. Understanding Threats

Threats are categorized by severity:

  • 🔴 High: Immediate risks like unauthorized network devices, potential spoofing, or proximity-based Bluetooth attacks (KNOB).
  • 🟠 Medium: Suspicious activities like RSSI anomalies (potential relay attacks) or detected AirTags.
  • 🔵 Low: System actions, such as when a device is blocked or a scan is completed successfully.

All history is preserved in the Logs tab.

6. Hardware Sensor Monitor

Access this via the Sensors tab. It provides raw data from your device's physical hardware:

  • Accelerometer/Gyroscope: Detects if your device is being moved, vibrated, or handled.
  • Ambient Light: Can signal if a device has been removed from a pocket or if a camera is being covered.
  • GPS: High-precision location tracking.
  • Magnetometer: Detects magnetic interference or nearby electronic devices.

7. AI Anomaly Detection

The application uses built-in logic to identify patterns that standard scanners miss:

  • RSSI Anomaly: If a known device's signal strength changes drastically without physical movement, it may indicate a signal relay attack.
  • Connection Frequency: If a device attempts to connect/disconnect dozens of times per minute, it is flagged as a potential brute-force or flooding attack.
  • Mass Discovery: If 5+ unknown devices appear at once, the AI flags a "Mass Discovery Event," common in surveillance or high-density scanning environments.

8. Advanced Linux/Android Features

On Linux and Rooted Android, the app can leverage system tools:

  • Nmap/Fping: For deep port scanning.
  • Bluetoothctl: To selectively block external MAC addresses.
  • Iwlist/Airodump: For detecting WiFi deauthentication attacks.

Note: These require the appropriate system packages (bluez, wireless-tools, nmap) to be installed on the host.

9. Troubleshooting

  • No Bluetooth Devices: Ensure Bluetooth is enabled on your device and permissions are granted.
  • Scan Failed: On Linux, ensure the user running the app has permissions for raw sockets (required for ping/nmap).
  • Location Not Updating: Check if GPS is enabled and the app has "High Accuracy" permissions.
  • High Battery Usage: Continuous monitoring of all sensors and network can be power-intensive. The app scans every 60 seconds by default to balance security and battery life.
